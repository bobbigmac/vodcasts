# AGENTS.md for `feeds/reach`

This folder is for outreach-ready tweet copy and related notes.
When writing or revising files here, prefer direct, usable output over polished explanation.

## What belongs here

- Tweet packs for reach / promotion.
- Alternate drafts and bad or rejected versions.
- Short working notes if they help produce better tweet copy.

## Core aim

Promote the `prays.be` library in a way that feels like an interested person sharing things they enjoy and find meaning in.
The writing should make people curious enough to click without sounding like ad copy, church PR, or AI filler.

## Voice

Write like a human with a steady, specific voice.

- No preamble.
- No helpful-explainer framing.
- No question restatement.
- No tidy headings or labels inside the tweet files unless explicitly asked.
- No canned openings or closers.
- Be concise and stop when the point lands.

The tone should feel approachable, observant, and lightly personal.
It can be mildly imperfect.
It should not sound polished for its own sake. 

## Avoid

- Generic hype.
- Marketing language.
- Buzzwords.
- "Journey", "landscape", or other inflated metaphors.
- Stock transitions.
- Symmetrical sentence templates.
- Repetitive cadence.
- Forced neutrality or both-sides padding.
- Dictionary-style explanation.
- Generic examples.
- Rhetorical flourishes.
- Poeticized comparisons.
- Emoji.
- Smart-quote flourishes.
- Em dashes.
- Over-hyphenation.
- Tricolons.
- Anaphora.
- Parataxis runs.
- The same sentence move repeated over and over.

Do not default to:

- observation -> neat interpretation -> tidy image
- problem -> lesson -> reassurance
- "this is really about..."
- "if you want..."
- "one of those..."
- "easy win"
- "great for"
- "nice place to start"

Those patterns go stale fast.

## Rhythm

Vary sentence jobs and paragraph shape.
Let the writing stall, jump, contradict itself a little, or quieten down if that helps it sound real.
Not every item needs the same posture.

Some tweets can be blunt.
Some can be warm.
Some can sound like a note to self.
Some can just point out why a line or theme stayed with you.

## Tweet file format

Unless asked otherwise, use this format:

- No heading.
- No numbering.
- One text block.
- Link on the very next line.
- Two blank lines between items.

Make the file easy to copy-paste into a scheduler.

## Link rules

- Use only `prays.be` links.
- Prefer the modern feedless route format.
- Do not use legacy `/feed/` URLs.
- For episode packs, link to the specific episode page.
- For show packs, use show pages or feed landing pages as appropriate.
- Only promote video-backed content.

## Curation rules

Lean toward safe, broad, mainstream church material.

- Prefer church services, sermon archives, Bible teaching, worship-adjacent Sunday content, and solid series pages.
- Avoid fringe, extreme, sensational, or obviously weird ministries.
- Avoid audio-only entries.
- Avoid support or bonus categories unless explicitly requested.
- Spread picks across the portfolio instead of overusing one channel.

## Episode-specific writing

When writing about an episode:

- Read the transcript if available.
- Check the cached feed item title and description.
- Write like you actually know what the episode is doing.
- Mention the issue, tension, or angle the episode raises.
- Prefer specifics over praise words.
- Make it sound like a short review or reaction, not a catalog entry.
- Talk about the content of the sermon, event, set, or conversation.
- Do not review the video itself or sell the production.
- Avoid lines that amount to "this video is great" or "I like this clip."

Do not just paraphrase the title.

## Show-specific writing

When writing about a show or feed:

- Treat it like a recommendation from somebody who has spent time browsing it.
- Mention the feel, pacing, or kind of material it contains.
- Avoid sounding like a product description.

## Working rule

If a draft starts sounding creepy, over-neat, or brochure-like, rewrite it plainer and cut the performance out of it.
